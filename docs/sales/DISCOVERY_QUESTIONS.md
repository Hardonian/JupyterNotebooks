# Discovery Questions Library

**Last Updated:** 2024-01-XX  
**Purpose:** Comprehensive library of discovery questions for sales conversations

---

## Overview

This library provides questions for different stages of the sales process and different customer segments.

**Question Categories:**
- Opening questions
- Problem discovery
- Solution fit
- Budget and authority
- Timeline and urgency
- Objection handling
- Closing questions

---

## Opening Questions

### Building Rapport

**Questions:**
- "Tell me about your role and responsibilities."
- "What does a typical day look like for you?"
- "How long have you been with [Company]?"
- "What are your biggest challenges right now?"

**Purpose:** Build rapport, understand context

---

### Understanding Current State

**Questions:**
- "What are you currently using for [use case]?"
- "How is that working for you?"
- "What do you like about your current solution?"
- "What would you change if you could?"

**Purpose:** Understand current situation

---

## Problem Discovery Questions

### Identifying Pain Points

**Questions:**
- "What's the biggest challenge you're facing?"
- "What's not working with your current approach?"
- "What keeps you up at night?"
- "If you could solve one problem, what would it be?"
- "What's the cost of the current problem?"

**Purpose:** Identify pain points and urgency

---

### Impact Questions

**Questions:**
- "How does this problem affect your team?"
- "What's the business impact?"
- "How much time/money does this cost you?"
- "What happens if this isn't solved?"
- "Who else is affected by this?"

**Purpose:** Quantify impact and urgency

---

### Root Cause Questions

**Questions:**
- "Why is this happening?"
- "What's the root cause?"
- "What have you tried to solve this?"
- "Why didn't previous solutions work?"
- "What would need to change?"

**Purpose:** Understand root causes

---

## Best Practices

### Question Techniques

**1. Open-Ended Questions**
- Start with "What," "How," "Tell me about"
- Avoid yes/no questions
- Encourage detailed responses

**2. Follow-Up Questions**
- "Can you tell me more about that?"
- "Why is that important?"
- "What does that mean for you?"

**3. Probing**
- "What I'm hearing is..."
- "So if I understand correctly..."
- "Let me make sure I have this right..."

---

### Question Sequencing

**1. Start Broad**
- "Tell me about your challenges"

**2. Narrow Down**
- "What's the biggest challenge?"

**3. Dig Deeper**
- "Why is that happening?"

**4. Quantify**
- "What's the impact?"

**5. Solution Fit**
- "How would this help?"

## Appendix: Quick Reference

### Discovery Call Structure

   - Rapport building
   - Current state

2. **Problem Discovery (15 min)**
   - Pain points
   - Impact
   - Root causes

3. **Solution Fit (10 min)**
   - Use case
   - Requirements
   - Success criteria

4. **Qualification (10 min)**
   - Budget
   - Authority
   - Timeline

5. **Closing (5 min)**
   - Summary
   - Next steps
