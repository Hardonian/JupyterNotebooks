# Job-Based User Research Plan - Agent Factory

**For:** Jobs-to-Be-Done, User Validation, Customer Development  
**Last Updated:** 2024-01-XX

---

## Overview

This document outlines a user research plan framed around Jobs-to-Be-Done. JTBD research focuses on understanding what customers are trying to accomplish, not just what features they want.

---

## Research Objectives

### Primary Objectives
1. **Validate Job Statement:** Confirm "deploy AI agent to production quickly"
2. **Understand Job Steps:** Map complete job journey
3. **Identify Switch Moments:** Understand when customers switch
4. **Validate Alternatives:** Understand current alternatives
5. **Measure Job Performance:** Understand how well we perform the job

### Secondary Objectives
6. **Identify Pain Points:** Understand job-related pain points
7. **Understand Emotional Jobs:** Understand emotional needs
8. **Validate Messaging:** Test job-based messaging

---

## Research Methodology

### Method 1: Job Interview

**Format:** One-on-one interviews (45-60 minutes)

**Participants:** 10-20 potential customers (solo founders, researchers, developers)

**Questions:**

**Opening (5 min):**
- "Tell me about yourself and what you're building."
- "What's your background in AI/ML?"

**Job Discovery (20 min):**
- "Tell me about the last time you tried to deploy an AI agent to production."
- "What were you trying to accomplish?"
- "What happened?"
- "What was the outcome?"

**Alternatives (10 min):**
- "What alternatives did you consider?"
- "Why did you choose [alternative]?"
- "What didn't work about [alternative]?"
- "What would make you switch?"

**Switch Moments (10 min):**
- "When did you realize you needed a different solution?"
- "What triggered you to look for alternatives?"
- "What made you switch?"

**Job Performance (10 min):**
- "How would you measure success?"
- "What would make this job easier?"
- "What's missing?"

**Closing (5 min):**
- "Anything else you'd like to share?"
- "Can we follow up with you?"

---

### Method 2: Survey

**Format:** Online survey (10-15 minutes)

**Participants:** 50-100 potential customers

**Questions:**

**Job Statement:**
- "When building an AI agent, what's your main goal?" (Multiple choice)
- "How important is deploying to production quickly?" (1-5 scale)

**Alternatives:**
- "What's your current approach to deploying AI agents?" (Multiple choice)
- "Why did you choose this approach?" (Open text)

**Pain Points:**
- "What's your biggest challenge when deploying AI agents?" (Multiple choice)
- "How painful is this challenge?" (1-5 scale)

**Switch Moments:**
- "What would make you switch to a different solution?" (Multiple choice)
- "When would you switch?" (Multiple choice)

**Job Performance:**
- "How do you measure success when deploying AI agents?" (Multiple choice)
- "What would make deployment easier?" (Open text)

---

### Method 3: Concierge Test

**Format:** Manual service (help users deploy agents)

**Participants:** 5-10 potential customers

**Process:**
1. Offer to help users deploy their agents manually
2. Walk through deployment process with them
3. Observe their reactions and pain points
4. Ask questions about their experience
5. Document learnings

**Learnings:**
- What are the actual pain points?
- What steps are confusing?
- What would make it easier?
- What's missing?

## Research Questions

### Job Discovery Questions

1. **"Tell me about the last time you tried to deploy an AI agent to production."**
   - Understand the job context
   - Identify job steps
   - Understand pain points

2. **"What were you trying to accomplish?"**
   - Understand functional job
   - Identify desired outcomes
   - Understand success criteria

3. **"What happened?"**
   - Understand job journey
   - Identify obstacles
   - Understand outcomes

4. **"What was the outcome?"**
   - Understand job success/failure
   - Identify satisfaction
   - Understand next steps

### Alternatives Questions

5. **"What alternatives did you consider?"**
   - Understand alternatives
   - Identify why alternatives chosen
   - Understand alternatives not chosen

6. **"Why did you choose [alternative]?"**
   - Understand decision criteria
   - Identify what matters
   - Understand trade-offs

7. **"What didn't work about [alternative]?"**
   - Understand pain points with alternatives
   - Identify gaps
   - Understand switch triggers

### Switch Moment Questions

8. **"When did you realize you needed a different solution?"**
   - Identify switch moments
   - Understand triggers
   - Understand timing

9. **"What triggered you to look for alternatives?"**
   - Understand switch triggers
   - Identify pain points
   - Understand urgency

10. **"What made you switch?"**
    - Understand switch criteria
    - Identify what matters
    - Understand decision factors

### Job Performance Questions

11. **"How would you measure success?"**
    - Understand success criteria
    - Identify metrics
    - Understand expectations

12. **"What would make this job easier?"**
    - Understand improvements needed
    - Identify features
    - Understand value

13. **"What's missing?"**
    - Understand gaps
    - Identify needs
    - Understand opportunities

## Research Participants

### Target Participants

**Segment 1: Solo Founders (5-10 participants)**
- Building AI-powered SaaS
- Have prototype working
- Need to deploy to production
- Limited budget/resources

**Segment 2: Researchers (3-5 participants)**
- AI/ML researchers
- Prototype in notebooks
- Want to deploy to production
- Technical background

**Segment 3: Product Teams (3-5 participants)**
- Product teams at companies
- Building AI features
- Need to deploy quickly
- Technical team

**Segment 4: Education (2-3 participants)**
- Education institutions
- Need FERPA compliance
- Want to deploy AI tools
- IT/technical team
