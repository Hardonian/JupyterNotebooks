# Product-Qualified Lead (PQL) Criteria - Agent Factory

**For:** Product-Led Growth, Sales Handoff, Enterprise Sales  
**Last Updated:** 2024-01-XX

---

## Overview

This document defines Product-Qualified Lead (PQL) criteria—when a free/self-serve user becomes qualified for sales-assisted upgrade to Enterprise tier. PQLs bridge product-led growth and sales-assisted growth.

---

## PQL Definition

**Product-Qualified Lead (PQL):** A free or self-serve user who demonstrates enterprise buying signals through product usage, indicating readiness for Enterprise tier and sales assistance.

---

## PQL Criteria

### Criterion 1: High Usage Volume

**Signal:** User consistently uses 80%+ of Pro tier limits (80K+ requests/month)

**Why It Matters:**
- High usage = high value
- Likely needs Enterprise features (unlimited, custom SLAs)
- Willing to pay more for higher limits

**Scoring:**
- **High (3 points):** 80%+ of Pro tier consistently
- **Medium (2 points):** 60-80% of Pro tier
- **Low (1 point):** <60% of Pro tier

---

### Criterion 2: Enterprise Features Usage

**Signal:** User uses or requests enterprise features (SSO, compliance, custom SLAs, dedicated support)

**Why It Matters:**
- Enterprise features = enterprise needs
- Indicates enterprise customer
- Willing to pay for enterprise features

**Scoring:**
- **High (3 points):** Uses/requests multiple enterprise features
- **Medium (2 points):** Uses/requests one enterprise feature
- **Low (1 point):** No enterprise feature usage

---

### Criterion 3: High Engagement

**Signal:** User is highly engaged (daily active, multiple agents, regular usage)

**Why It Matters:**
- High engagement = high value
- Likely to expand usage
- Good fit for Enterprise tier

**Scoring:**
- **High (3 points):** Daily active, 10+ agents, regular usage
- **Medium (2 points):** Weekly active, 5-10 agents, moderate usage
- **Low (1 point):** Monthly active, <5 agents, low usage

---

### Criterion 4: Multiple Users/Team

**Signal:** Multiple users from same organization (detected via email domain, team features)

**Why It Matters:**
- Multiple users = team/enterprise
- Likely needs team features (SSO, RBAC)
- Higher revenue potential

**Scoring:**
- **High (3 points):** 10+ users from same domain
- **Medium (2 points):** 3-10 users from same domain
- **Low (1 point):** 1-2 users from same domain

---

### Criterion 5: Support Requests

**Signal:** User requests enterprise-level support (custom features, dedicated support, SLAs)

**Why It Matters:**
- Support requests = enterprise needs
- Indicates willingness to pay for support
- Good fit for Enterprise tier

**Scoring:**
- **High (3 points):** Multiple enterprise support requests
- **Medium (2 points):** One enterprise support request
- **Low (1 point):** No enterprise support requests

---

### Criterion 6: Education/Enterprise Domain

**Signal:** User email domain indicates education institution or enterprise (edu, large company domain)

**Why It Matters:**
- Education/enterprise domains = enterprise customers
- Likely needs enterprise features (compliance, SSO)
- Higher revenue potential

**Scoring:**
- **High (3 points):** Education (.edu) or large enterprise domain
- **Medium (2 points):** Medium enterprise domain
- **Low (1 point):** Personal/small business domain

---

## PQL Scoring

### Scoring System
- **Total Score:** Sum of all criterion scores (max 18 points)
- **PQL Threshold:** 12+ points (High PQL)
- **Medium PQL:** 8-11 points (Monitor, may become PQL)
- **Low PQL:** <8 points (Not PQL)

### PQL Levels

**High PQL (12+ points):**
- Ready for Enterprise sales outreach
- High probability of Enterprise conversion
- Prioritize for sales team

**Medium PQL (8-11 points):**
- Monitor for PQL signals
- May become High PQL
- Light touch (email, in-product prompts)

**Low PQL (<8 points):**
- Not PQL yet
- Continue product-led growth
- No sales outreach

## PQL Identification

### Automated Identification
- **Telemetry:** Track usage, features, engagement
- **Scoring:** Calculate PQL score automatically
- **Alerts:** Alert sales team when PQL identified

### Manual Identification
- **Sales Team:** Review high-usage accounts
- **Support Team:** Flag enterprise support requests
- **Product Team:** Identify enterprise feature usage

---

## PQL Handoff Process

### Step 1: PQL Identified
- System identifies PQL (automated or manual)
- PQL score calculated
- PQL added to sales pipeline

### Step 2: Sales Outreach
- Sales team contacts PQL
- Personalized outreach based on usage
- Offer Enterprise tier and features

### Step 3: Qualification
- Sales team qualifies PQL
- Understand needs and budget
- Determine Enterprise fit

### Step 4: Proposal
- Sales team creates Enterprise proposal
- Custom pricing and features
- Present to PQL

### Step 5: Close
- Negotiate Enterprise contract
- Close Enterprise deal
- Onboard Enterprise customer

## PQL Metrics

### PQL Identification Metrics
- **PQLs Identified:** Number of PQLs identified
- **PQL Conversion Rate:** % of PQLs → Enterprise customers
- **Time to PQL:** Average time from signup to PQL
- **PQL by Source:** PQLs by acquisition channel

### PQL Sales Metrics
- **PQL → Enterprise Conversion:** % of PQLs converting to Enterprise
- **PQL → Enterprise Time:** Average time from PQL to Enterprise
- **PQL → Enterprise Revenue:** Revenue from PQL → Enterprise conversions
- **PQL → Enterprise LTV:** Lifetime value of PQL → Enterprise customers
